Summer Watermelon Gazpacho

\

Description

Summer Watermelon Gazpacho is a refreshing and vibrant cold soup that combines the sweetness of watermelon with the savory flavors of traditional gazpacho ingredients. This chilled soup is perfect for hot summer days, as it provides a cooling and hydrating experience. The base of the gazpacho is made with ripe watermelon, which is pureed until smooth, resulting in a silky and luscious texture. Watermelon's natural sweetness adds a delightful twist to the traditional gazpacho recipe.

Along with the watermelon, Summer Watermelon Gazpacho incorporates other fresh ingredients such as tomatoes, cucumbers, bell peppers, and red onions. These ingredients add a variety of flavors and textures to the soup. Tomatoes contribute a tangy and slightly acidic taste, while cucumbers provide a crisp and refreshing crunch. Bell peppers and red onions add a subtle spiciness and a hint of bite. The soup is typically seasoned with a combination of olive oil, vinegar (often sherry vinegar), garlic, salt, and pepper, which enhance the overall taste and bring all the flavors together. The result is a chilled soup bursting with the sweetness of watermelon and the vibrant flavors of summer vegetables, creating a harmonious and refreshing culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups cubed seeded watermelon
  • 2 roma (plum) tomatoes, seeded and chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • ½ English (seedless) cucumber - peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons minced shallot
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt, or to taste
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons minced fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Combine watermelon, tomatoes, red pepper, cucumber, shallot, lime juice, vinegar, salt, and pepper in a high-powered blender. Blend until smooth, 20 to 30 seconds. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow flavors to meld and soup to chill.

  2. Stir gazpacho before serving, as it has a tendency to separate, and divide between 4 bowls. Top with feta cheese and cilantro.

  3. To seed tomatoes, cut in half horizontally and gently squeeze to release seeds and excess liquid.
